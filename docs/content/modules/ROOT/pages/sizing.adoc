= Sizing Guide
:navtitle: Sizing Guide

This page helps you estimate how many vectors fit in a given memory budget and pick an
index / quantizer combination that matches your dataset and SLO.

== Memory model

Vectors uses two memory pools:

* **Heap** -- Java objects, HNSW/Vamana graph nodes, in-memory vector arrays
  (`float[][]`). Bounded by `-Xmx`.
* **Off-heap (`MemorySegment`)** -- memory-mapped `vectors.bin` and `graph.bin` files used
  by persistent collections. Bounded by RAM plus OS page-cache behaviour, not by `-Xmx`.

All calculations below assume the **in-memory** (heap-resident) configuration unless
noted. Persistent/mmap-backed collections lift the per-vector heap cost to roughly a few
dozen bytes per node (graph adjacency) and push the float payload off-heap.

== Per-vector cost by encoding

The dominant cost is the vector payload. For dimension `dim`:

[cols="2,2,3", options="header"]
|===
| Encoding | Bytes per vector | Notes
| `FLOAT32`        | `4 × dim`       | Full precision; reference.
| `INT8` (scalar)  | `1 × dim` + 8   | 1 byte per element + per-vector `{min, scale}`.
| `BINARY` (BQ)    | `dim / 8`       | 1 bit per element, rounded up to the nearest byte.
|===

For rescore-style configurations (quantized navigator + full-precision retained for exact
rescore via `IndexRefine`), add the `FLOAT32` cost on top of the navigator encoding.

== Per-node index overhead

Graph indexes add adjacency lists per node. Approximate overhead in bytes:

[cols="2,3,3", options="header"]
|===
| Index     | Formula                            | Example
| `FLAT`    | `0`                                | no graph
| `HNSW`    | `≈ 12 × M`                         | M=16 → ~192 B; M=32 → ~384 B
| `VAMANA`  | `≈ 4 × R`                          | R=32 → ~128 B; R=64 → ~256 B
| `IVF_FLAT`| `≈ 4 × k` (centroid ids only)      | k=√n; tiny per vector
|===

The `12 × M` factor for HNSW reflects level-0 adjacency (`2 × M` neighbours × 4 bytes)
plus upper-level contributions amortised across the node population. Vamana is single-layer.

== Capacity tables (heap-resident)

Usable heap is approximately `-Xmx × 0.75` after GC and JVM overhead. The tables below
give the vector count at full utilisation for three common dimensions: `384`
(MiniLM / E5-small), `768` (BERT / OpenAI ada-002-small), and `1536` (OpenAI ada-002).

=== FLAT + FLOAT32

[cols="1,1,1,1", options="header"]
|===
| dim | `-Xmx 4G` | `-Xmx 8G` | `-Xmx 16G`
| 384  | ~2.0 M | ~4.0 M | ~8.0 M
| 768  | ~1.0 M | ~2.0 M | ~4.0 M
| 1536 | ~0.5 M | ~1.0 M | ~2.0 M
|===

=== HNSW (M=16) + FLOAT32

Per vector: `4 × dim + 192` bytes.

[cols="1,1,1,1", options="header"]
|===
| dim | `-Xmx 4G` | `-Xmx 8G` | `-Xmx 16G`
| 384  | ~1.85 M | ~3.7 M | ~7.4 M
| 768  | ~0.93 M | ~1.85 M | ~3.7 M
| 1536 | ~0.48 M | ~0.97 M | ~1.95 M
|===

=== HNSW (M=16) + INT8 (quantized navigator, no rescore vectors)

Per vector: `dim + 200` bytes.

[cols="1,1,1,1", options="header"]
|===
| dim | `-Xmx 4G` | `-Xmx 8G` | `-Xmx 16G`
| 384  | ~5.2 M  | ~10.4 M | ~20.8 M
| 768  | ~3.1 M  | ~6.2 M  | ~12.4 M
| 1536 | ~1.75 M | ~3.5 M  | ~7.0 M
|===

=== HNSW (M=16) + INT8 navigator + FLOAT32 rescore (`IndexRefine`)

Per vector: `4 × dim + dim + 200` bytes ≈ `5 × dim + 200`.

[cols="1,1,1,1", options="header"]
|===
| dim | `-Xmx 4G` | `-Xmx 8G` | `-Xmx 16G`
| 384  | ~1.48 M | ~2.95 M | ~5.9 M
| 768  | ~0.75 M | ~1.50 M | ~3.0 M
| 1536 | ~0.38 M | ~0.76 M | ~1.52 M
|===

=== HNSW (M=16) + Binary (BQ) navigator + FLOAT32 rescore

Per vector: `4 × dim + dim/8 + 200`.

[cols="1,1,1,1", options="header"]
|===
| dim | `-Xmx 4G` | `-Xmx 8G` | `-Xmx 16G`
| 384  | ~1.87 M | ~3.75 M | ~7.5 M
| 768  | ~0.94 M | ~1.88 M | ~3.75 M
| 1536 | ~0.48 M | ~0.96 M | ~1.92 M
|===

== Choosing a configuration

[cols="2,3,3", options="header"]
|===
| Corpus size | In-memory choice                  | Persistent / mmap choice
| `< 100K`    | `FLAT` (exact)                    | `FLAT`
| `100K–10M`  | `HNSW` M=16 + FLOAT32             | `VAMANA` (mmap) or `HNSW` mmap
| `10M–100M`  | `HNSW` + INT8 + rescore           | `VAMANA` + INT8 rescore
| `> 100M`    | Shards (`IndexShards`) of the above or `IVF_FLAT` | `VAMANA` mmap + shards
|===

== Tuning knobs

* **HNSW `M`** -- higher `M` increases recall but raises per-node overhead by `~12 × M`
  bytes. Typical range: `16–48`.
* **Vamana `R`** -- graph degree; similar effect to HNSW `M`. Typical range: `32–64`.
* **`overQueryFactor`** -- coarse-pass over-fetch; recall tuning without rebuild.
* **`searchMultiStart`** -- intra-query parallelism for HNSW via virtual threads; raises
  QPS at high recall at the cost of extra CPU per query.
* **IVF `nprobe`** -- number of clusters scanned; controls recall/latency trade.

== Off-heap (mmap) note

For persistent collections the `vectors.bin` payload is zero-copy via `MemorySegment`
over mmap. Heap pressure drops to the graph adjacency lists plus per-document metadata;
the OS page cache is the effective capacity ceiling. See the Getting Started guide for
enabling `PersistentVectorCollection`.
